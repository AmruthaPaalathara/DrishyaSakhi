Why Drishya Sakhi?

Drishya Sakhi enables visually impaired users to comprehend visual content such as images and videos, promoting independence and confidence in navigating their surroundings. Unlike existing models that focus on English, Drishya Sakhi generates captions in Hindi, ensuring inclusivity for a wider audience, particularly in linguistically diverse regions like India. The dual functionality of text-to-voice conversion in Hindi ensures visually impaired users receive intuitive auditory descriptions, making the technology user-friendly and accessible.

To whom Drishya Sakhi is?

The Drishya Sakhi project is designed for visually impaired individuals, elderly people with low eyesight, and non-English speakers in India who face challenges in accessing and comprehending visual content. It primarily serves those who struggle to navigate their surroundings, understand images, and interact with visual media due to vision-related impairments or language barriers. Additionally, the project benefits educational institutions, assistive technology developers, and organizations working toward accessibility and inclusivity for differently-abled individuals, ensuring that they can live more independent, empowered, and fulfilling lives.
